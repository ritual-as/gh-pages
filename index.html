Hello! I’m misha (she/her). 

Ritual As is an altar at which people are invited to gather towards grounding and connecting with self and community in ways that interrupt the constant onslaught of capitalism on our minds, bodies, and spirits.

At this altar, I offer my skills as an embodiment facilitator towards co-creating learning spaces that support us in: meeting the moment, feeling more like ourselves, and snatching back energy from colonial systems to build more of what's important to us.

For individuals: I support people in bringing comfort, support, and ease to the the process of learning and navigating our body's landscape of stress responses--cultivating a practice of self-awareness as the foundation for the agency to enact sustainable change.
I'm your co-explorer in charting your ecosystem of stress and relaxation--we start gently with noticing, observing, and tending to what's present.

For organizations: Together we co-craft a space to bring these same skills and outcomes to your staff, community, or class participants. I offer end-to-end event support in consulting, designing, planning, administrating, producing, and hosting trauma-informed somatic facilitations for your organization.

Beyond being a somatic practitioner, I am a person showing up to do what I can, when I can, with what I have and who I'm with to throw sand in the nearest gears of oppressive systems--and my goal is to be a co-conspiritor with you in doing the same.

I will be here to equip individuals and our communities with quick, accessible somatic practices for stabilizing, reconnecting, and growing our capacity for healing and transformation. 

Ritual As is my reminder to myself and my community that magic--power, spirit, change, however you personally call it--is in the every day ways we relate to our every day spaces. Our minds and bodies are our 24/7 everyday spaces. Our rituals for how we navigate, support, and steward those spaces (or not) are in effect for every moment of our lives, with their impacts compounding whether they're tended to or not.
